This is an exercise modeled after a real-world training scenario used in the past by Procter & Gamble to train U.S. employees on the concept of “Category Management”.

You are the ones “asking” the questions, and are what is commonly referred to in the CPG industry as “Business Development Managers” for Washaway Clean (WAC). Your relationship to Trojan Grocer (TG) is a selling relationship, but also a “consultative” one (i.e., you offer advice to increase profitability and sales in this highly margin-conscious industry).

Category Management is the process of managing the 4P’s of a product category in a manner that maximizes sales and profitability. The intent of all Category Management projects is to create mutually beneficial gains for both the manufacturer (e.g., Procter & Gamble) and the distributor (e.g., a retailer like Target).

As you assume the role of Business Managers for Washaway Clean, you should be aware of the fact that they are operating in a B2B setting (the relationship is between WAC and TG).


Washaway Clean - The Team Leader

Team Leaders, who are responsible for sales results in a specific region or channel, have a wide variety of backgrounds and personalities. They do, however, share some common characteristics such as: strong analytical abilities, excellent negotiating skills, a talent for strategic planning, and a strong drive to achieve results.

In this case, your Team Leader is a young, “up-and-coming” executive who will soon rise through the ranks to manage WAC sales for a bigger, national retailer (a chain such as Target).

Over time, his drive and his ability to achieve results will help him rise to positions with even more responsibility (Associate Director of West Coast Sales, for example). Despite his young age, he has a great deal of knowledge about this industry, which he acquired through experience as well as through his education as a business major in college.


Trojan Grocer - In-House Marketing Specialist

Margaret Atway is Trojan Grocer's In-House Marketing Specialist. Her role requires Margaret to address a variety of matters related to the 4Ps, but her main responsibility is to create consumer marketing programs that will be executed in Trojan Grocer's stores.

Margaret aspires to the role of VP of Marketing for the chain.


Washaway Clean - Brand Manager

As Washaway Clean brand manager, Peter Lane, is responsible for positioning a brand in the market, identifying the right target market, and maintaining the good reputation of a brand. He focuses on every aspect of the 4P’s as they pertain to a specific brand: the product itself, the price, how to promote it, etc.

A brand manager works on a team with assistant brand managers, finance experts, logistics experts, etc. to make decisions that impact the brand.


Trojan Grocer - V.P. of Acquisitions

As VP of Acquisitions for Trojan Grocer, Danny Ortega makes decisions about what to sell in his stores. His decisions are based on extensive analysis of market trends, as well as, internal secondary data such as scanner data. In this case, for example, Danny will tell you that he expects your suggestions about product assortment to be solidly backed by data.


Washaway Clean - Consumer Marketing Knowledge Expert

A Consumer Marketing Knowledge (CMK) expert for Washaway, like Janet Ferguson, is very familiar with consumer behavior. Someone in Janet’s role, for example, would be able to discuss demographic segmentation data with you. She would also be able to tell you how consumers relate to certain brands, why consumers are loyal to one brand vs. another, and how consumers make purchase decisions.

A CMK expert also knows how to interpret panel data and probably has experience running consumer behavior studies including focus groups.
